Freshman Year

The experience that has had the most impact on my academic and professional future this year was deciding to switch my major. Starting college, I thought there was no way that I was going to change my major; I had been telling people that I was going to become an aerospace engineer since middle school. However, when sitting in my Intro to Aerospace Engineering class this spring, I quickly realized that it was not the right path for me. I realized that I was completely fascinated with space and how the universe works, but not as interested in the machines that get humans into space. So I decided to switch my major to astrophysics, which completely changed how my college experience and timeline will be.

Instead of taking five years to get my degree and having to spend five semesters doing co-ops, my college path is more straight-forward, only taking four years to get my degree with no required co-ops. However, I believe that getting real-life experience is still very important. So now one of my goals is to get involved in a physics research project as an undergraduate student. Additionally, as an engineering major, I was not sure if I wanted to go to graduate school. However, as an astrophysics major, I now plan on going graduate school, most likely to get my PhD (which going to school for another 6 or so years is crazy but exciting). I am hoping that doing a research experience before I graduation will help me figure out if I want to go to graduate school to continue doing research.

Another experience that potentially changed my professional goals is helping my peers study for their math and physics classes. I felt like I was good at explaining concepts to my friends and I enjoyed helping them learn information. I found that I might be interested in becoming a physics teacher or professor. To further determine whether or not I will enjoy teaching, I plan on volunteering through the Bearcat Buddies program this year. Through this program, I would tutor students at Cincinnati schools. Additionally, I want to look into becoming an SI leader or recitation instructor in the next couple of years.

Overall, my first year was very different than I expected (in addition to there being a pandemic). I never thought I would change my major but I am super excited for the future and I am ready to dive deep into the world of physics.
